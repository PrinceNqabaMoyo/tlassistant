## **Alternative Drawing Task Approaches**

### **4. Construction Tasks (No Precision Required)**
- "Draw a line parallel to this one"
- "Draw a line perpendicular to this one"

- "Make this quadrilateral a square"

### **5. Categorical Drawing Tasks**
- "Draw a triangle that looks acute"
- "Draw a quadrilateral that looks like a rectangle"
- "Make this shape look more like a square"




## **The Precision Problem Remains**

Even with "parallel" lines, we still have the same issue:
- How parallel is "parallel enough"?
- Different screen sizes will affect perception
- Students might draw lines that are "visually parallel" but not mathematically perfect

## **Alternative Approaches to Avoid Precision Issues**

### **1. Binary Choice Tasks Instead of Drawing**
Instead of "Draw a parallel line", use:
- "Click on the line that is parallel to this one"
- "Select which line is perpendicular"
- "Choose the correct completion of this triangle"

### **2. Template-Based Drawing**
- Provide pre-drawn elements that students can drag/place
- "Drag this line to make it parallel"
- "Place this angle to complete the triangle"
- "Select the correct side length from these options"

### **3. Visual Recognition Tasks**
- "Click on all the right angles in this shape"
- "Select which sides are equal"
- "Identify the parallel sides"

### **4. Construction with Guides**
- Show grid lines or guides
- Provide snap-to-grid functionality
- Use visual indicators (like in CAD programs)
