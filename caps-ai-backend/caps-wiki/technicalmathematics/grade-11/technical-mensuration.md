### Surface Area of Prisms

NOTE: TOOL REQUIRED to render 3D prisms in explanations and questions, and users should be able to input parameter to generate a prism in answers and workings. In answers, the user is asked to select the 3d shape they want to use, they are not given name of the 3d shapes because they have to know them.

A 3-D object is made up of a

number of surfaces, thus when we refer to the area of a 3-D object, we refer to the surface area.

To calculate the surface area of a 3-D object, we need to calculate the total of the areas of all the

surfaces of the object. The unit of measure for surface area is a square unit, for example, m^2.

A prism is a type of three-dimensional (3-D) object that has flat sides. It has two ends that are the

same shape and size (and look like a 2-D shape). Here are some examples of prisms:

 DIAGRAM: Yellow Cube

 DIAGRAM: Blue Rectangular Prism (Cuboid)

 DIAGRAM: Magenta Triangular Prism

 DIAGRAM: Orange Pentagonal Prism

 DIAGRAM: Red Hexagonal Prism

 DIAGRAM: Green Octagonal Prism

Each prism has the same cross-section all along the shape from end to end. This means that if we

cut through the prism, we would see the same 2-D shape on either end. That is why we use a special

formula to calculate surface area.

To find the surface area of a prism, use the formula:

Surface area of a prism = (2 × base area) + (base perimeter × length or height of prism)

### The effect that changing dimensions will have surface area

When changing the dimensions of an object, there is no simple numeric relationship between the original surface area and the new surface area. However, if all the dimensions are increased by the same factor (k), the new surface area becomes: k2 × original area.

##### Example

A cuboid has the dimensions: l = 2 cm, b = 1 cm and h = 6 cm.  What effect will a change in one of the dimensions have on the surface area of the prism?

Using the original dimensions:

 Surface area of a cuboid = 2 × area of base + base perimeter × height

 = 2 × (2 × 1) + (2 + 1 + 2 + 1) × 6

 = 4 + 36

 = 40 cm^2

 If the dimensions are each multiplied by a constant factor of 5:

 Length becomes: 5 × 2 = 10 cm

 Surface area becomes:   = 2 × (10 × 1) + (10 + 1 + 10 + 1) × 6

 = 20 + 132

 = 152 cm^2

 Original surface area  = 40 cm^2

 New surface area  = 152 cm^2

 Breadth becomes: 5 × 1 = 5 cm

 Surface area becomes: = 2 × (2 × 5) + (2 + 5 + 2 + 5) × 6

 = 20 + 84

 = 104 cm^2

 Original surface area  = 40 cm^2

 New surface area  = 104 cm^2

 Height becomes:  5 × 6 = 30 cm

 Surface area becomes: =2 × (2 × 1) + (2 + 1 + 2 + 1) × 30

 = 4 + 180

 = 184 cm^2

 Original surface area  = 40 cm^2

 New surface area  = 184 cm^2

 The new surface area is different each time.
