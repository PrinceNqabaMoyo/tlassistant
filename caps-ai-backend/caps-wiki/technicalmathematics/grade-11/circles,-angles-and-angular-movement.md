## Circles, angles and angular movement

### Circle

### Parts of a circle

&nbsp;• arc –  A portion of the circumference

&nbsp;• centre of a circle – the point from all points on a circle that is the same distance apart

&nbsp;• chord – a segment whose endpoints are on the circumference of the circle

&nbsp;• circumference – The length of the boundary. It is the circle’s perimeter

&nbsp;• secant – a line that intersects the circle at two points

&nbsp;• diameter – a chord that passes through the centre of the circle

&nbsp;• radius – a line segment from the centre of a circle to the circumference of the circle

&nbsp;• vertex – is the point where two or more line segments (or edges) meet.

### Circular measure

&nbsp;Area, A = πr2, where r is the radius.

Circumference, C = 2πr, where r is the radius or  C = πd, where d is the diameter).

### Inscribed angle

* An inscribed angle is an angle whose vertex is on a circle and whose sides contain chords of the circle. 
* An intercepted arc contains endpoints that lie on the sides on an inscribed angle and all the points of the circle between them. 
* A chord or arc subtends an angle if its endpoints lie on the sides of the angle.

Complete revolution:  360° = 2π radians; 180° = π radians

The radian measure, θ, of a central angle is the ratio of 

the arc length, s, the angle subtended by the radius of 

the circle, r.

 •  If an arc of length s is intercepted by a central angle θ (in radians) the angle subtended will be equal to the arc divided by the radius:

  θ= s/r .

• If s = r then θ = 1 radian.

• Convert degrees to radians and vice versa.

 

The relationship between the radius and arc length is linear, with a slope (gradient) of  2πr (60°/360°   )= π/3   , or about 1,05. 

The slope represents the ratio of an arc length to radius. 

This ratio is the radian measure of the angle, so 60° is the same as π/3 radians.

If the central angle θ of radius r intercepts an arc of  length r, then the measure of θ is defined as 1 radian. 

Since the circumference of a circle of radius r is 2πr, an angle representing one complete revolution measure is 2π radians, or 360°.

### Length of a circular arc 

An angle whose radian measure is θ is subtended by an arc that is the fraction, θ/2r, of the 

circumference of a circle. Thus, in a circle of radius r, the length s of an arc that subtends the angle θ is:

&nbsp;s = θ/2π \* circumference of a circle

&nbsp;  = θ/2π\*(2πr)

&nbsp;  = rθ

### The relationship between a segment’s height, diameter and its width

The relationship can be set out in the formula:  4*h*^2 – 4*dh* + *x*^2 = 0,

where *h* is the height of the segment, *d* is the diameter and *x* is the width of the segment.

### Angular and circumferential/peripheral velocity

A particle’s motion can be measured using its angular velocity (also known as its linear velocity). Angular velocity, ω (omega) can be determined by multiplying the radians in one revolution (2π) with the revolutions per second (n)

 	ω = radians per revolution \* revolution per second = radians per second =2πn = 360°n

Circumferential velocity is the linear velocity of a point on the circumference.

v = πDn with D the diameter, n is the rotation frequency.

The relationship between angular velocity and circumferential (linear) velocity is given below:

&nbsp;ω = 2πn

&nbsp;n = ω/2π ...(1)

&nbsp;v = πDn

&nbsp;n = v/πD ...(2)

&nbsp;v/πD = ω/2π

&nbsp;    v= ωπD/2π

&nbsp;     = ωD/2

D/2 = r

∴ v = ωr

The unit for measuring linear velocity is metre per second.
