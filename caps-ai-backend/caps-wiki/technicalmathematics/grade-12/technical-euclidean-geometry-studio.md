### Proportion

A proportion is an equation that equates two ratios. In proportion, each number is called a

term, for example,

In 3:4 = 9:12, 3 is the 1st term, 4 is the 2nd, 9 is the 3rd term and 12 is the 4th term.

The first and fourth terms are referred to as the extremes of the proportion; the second and third terms are referred to as the means of the proportion.

 

Note

The product of the means equals the product of the extremes.

 If a/b = c/d, then  a × d = b × c.

#### Properties of proportion

Stated differently, in a proportion, the two cross products are equal.

If a/b = c /d and a,b,c,d ≠ 0 , then each of the following is true.

 1.  ad = bc

2\.   d/b = c/a  (switch extremes)

3\.   a/c = b/d  (switch means)

4\.   b/a = d/c  (invert)

5\.   (a+b)/b = (d+c)/c

6\.   (a-b)/b = (d-c)/c

7\. If triangles are on the same base (or equal bases) and are between the same parallel lines,

then the triangles are equal in area.

Theorem

A line parallel to one side of a triangle divides the other two  sides (internally or externally) into segments that has the  same proportion.

### Similar Figures

 Similar figures have the same shape, but the size is not necessarily the same.

Two polygons are similar (|||) if:

a) corresponding angles are equal, and

b) ratios of lengths of corresponding sides are equal.

To dilate an object is to change its size (that is, to enlarge or reduce it) without changing its form. The changed (enlarged or reduced) object is called a dilatation or scale drawing of the original.

We write  QRST|||ABCD  to indicate that polygon QRST is similar to polygon ABCD.

The vertices in a similarity statement must be listed in corresponding order.

Theorems

*  If two triangles are equiangular, their corresponding sides are in the same proportion and thus are similar, i.e If two angles of a triangle are equal to two corresponding
* angles of another triangle, then the triangles are similar.
* If two triangles have their corresponding sides in the same proportion, then they are equiangular, and thus are similar
