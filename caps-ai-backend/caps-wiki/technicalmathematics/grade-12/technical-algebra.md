### Division of polynomials (Tool)

Dividend = Quotient \* Divisor + Remainder

To divide 3x^3 - 4x^2 + 5x - 6 by x-2

step1- Divide the 1st term in the Dividend by the 1st term in the divisor i.e 3x^3 √Ј x = 3x^2

step2- Multiply the divisor by the result from step 1, i.e 3x^2 \* (x-2) = 3x^3 - 6x^2

step3- Substract  result of step 2 from the dividend, i.e (3x^3 - 4x^2 + 5x - 6) - (3x^3 - 6x^2) = 2x^2+5x-6.

step4- Divide the result of step3 by the 1st term of the divisor and repeat the steps.

Final answer = 3x^2 + 2x + 9 + 12/(x-2)

this implies that: 3x^3 - 4x^2 + 5x - 6 = \[3x^2 + 2x + 9 + 12/(x-2)] \* (x-2)
