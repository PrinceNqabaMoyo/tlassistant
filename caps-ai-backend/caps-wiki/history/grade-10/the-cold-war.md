# Grade 10 History: The Cold War

## Overview
The Cold War was a period of geopolitical tension between the Soviet Union and the United States and their respective allies, the Eastern Bloc and the Western Bloc, after World War II.

## Key Concepts
1. **Ideological Conflict**: Capitalism (USA) vs. Communism (USSR).
2. **Arms Race**: Nuclear weapons development and the doctrine of Mutually Assured Destruction (MAD).
3. **Proxy Wars**: Conflicts in Korea, Vietnam, and Afghanistan where superpowers supported opposing sides.

## Timeline (1947–1991)
- **1947**: Truman Doctrine announced.
- **1948–1949**: Berlin Blockade and Airlift.
- **1950–1953**: Korean War.
- **1962**: Cuban Missile Crisis.
- **1965–1973**: Vietnam War escalation.
- **1989**: Fall of the Berlin Wall.
- **1991**: Dissolution of the Soviet Union.

## South African Context
South Africa's anti-communist stance aligned with Western powers during the Cold War, influencing domestic apartheid-era propaganda and foreign policy.
