# Grade 10 Economics: Economic Systems

## Overview
An economic system is the means by which societies allocate resources and distribute goods and services.

## Types of Economic Systems
1. **Traditional Economy**: Based on customs, beliefs, and traditions. Decisions are based on what has been done in the past.
2. **Market Economy**: Driven by supply and demand with minimal government intervention.
3. **Command Economy**: Central government makes all economic decisions.
4. **Mixed Economy**: Combines elements of market and command economies. Most modern economies, including South Africa, are mixed.

## South African Context
South Africa operates as a **mixed economy**. The government provides public services (education, healthcare, infrastructure) while private enterprise drives much of the industrial and service sectors.

## Key Indicators
- **GDP (Gross Domestic Product)**: Total value of goods and services produced.
- **Inflation**: Rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services rises.
- **Unemployment**: Percentage of the labour force that is jobless and actively seeking employment.
