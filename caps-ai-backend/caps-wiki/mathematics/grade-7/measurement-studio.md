### area and perimeter of 2d shapes

What is different to Grade 6?

&nbsp;• In Grade 6 learners did not have to use formulae to calculate area and 

perimeter.

&nbsp;•	Formulae learners should know and use are:

&nbsp;-perimeter of a square = 4s

&nbsp;-perimeter of a rectangle = 2(l + b) or 2l + 2b

&nbsp;-area of a square = l^2

&nbsp;-area of a rectangle = l x b

&nbsp;-area of a triangle = 1/2 (b x h)

### surface area and volume of 3d objects

&nbsp;What is different to Grade 6?

&nbsp;• In Grade 6 learners did not have to use formulae to calculate surface area and 

volume.

&nbsp;•	Formulae learners should know and use:

&nbsp;-the volume of a prism = the area of the base x the height

&nbsp;-the surface area of a prism = the sum of the area of all its faces

&nbsp;-the volume of a cube = l^3

&nbsp;-the volume of a rectangular prism = l x b x h

&nbsp;•	For conversions, note: 

&nbsp;-if 1 cm = 10 cm then 1 cm^3 = 1 000 mm^3 and 

&nbsp;-if 1 m = 10 cm then 1 m^3 = 1 000 000 mm^3 or 1 000 000 or 10^6 cm^3.

&nbsp;-an object with a volume of 1 cm^3  will displace exactly 1 ml of water; and 

&nbsp;-an object with a volume of 1 m^3  will displace exactly 1 kl of water.

&nbsp;• Emphasize that the amount of space inside a prism is called its capacity; and 

the amount of space occupied by a prism is called its volume.

&nbsp;• Investigate the nets of cubes and rectangular prisms in order to deduce 

formulae for calculating their surface areas.
