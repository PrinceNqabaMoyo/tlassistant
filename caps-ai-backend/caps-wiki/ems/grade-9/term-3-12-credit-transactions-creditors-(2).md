# Credit transactions: Creditors (2)

## Curriculum and Assessment Policy Statement (CAPS) content

In this topic, learners will learn about FINANCIAL LITERACY.

They will look at:

* recording of payments to creditors in the Cash Payments Journal (CPJ)
* posting to the Creditors Ledger and General Ledger
* the effect of credit transactions on the accounting equation

### New	words and concepts

cheques; counterfoils; signing rights; Creditors Ledger; General Ledger; Creditors List; creditors’ accounts; Creditors Control account

## Notes

* The learners already know how to record transactions in a General 
* Ledger. Posting from the CJ to the General Ledger should be fairly easy for the learners to master because it is done in the same way as posting from the CPJ. 
* Learners are familiar with a three-column ledger and they also know how to post journals to the Debtors Ledger, which looks similar to the Creditors Ledger. This should help their understanding of this content. 
* It may be difficult for them to understand that you have two 
* ‘three-column ledgers’ that look exactly the same, but function in exactly the opposite way to each other. Consider how you will explain this to learners.
* Continue with recording payments to creditors in the CPJ
* Discuss the steps from the point where the business places the 
* order until the point where the order is paid.

### A

* Talk about the accounting cycle and explain each step as you  

go along.

1. Source document – Cheque: Show learners a cheque on the board and remind them how to complete it.
2. Journal – Cash Payments Journal: Show learners how to allocate that cheque payment in the journal, in the Bank and in the Creditors control columns.

Explain that the Creditor’s Ledger differs from the General Ledger 

in that it is updated as soon as a transaction takes place. The 

ledger account then shows the balance due after each transaction, 

while the General Ledger shows only the closing balance at the 

end of the month.

### B

Discuss posting the Creditors Journal to the General Ledger  

for creditors, continuing with the example of Chelsea Traders. 

There are two ways to explain this:

1. The Creditors Control account has been credited because it increased (liabilities increase on the credit side) and according to the double-entry principle, all the other accounts are debited OR
2. Identify each account, for example: Trading Stock is an  Asset that increased because we bought some stock or Stationery is an expense that decreased Owner’s equity and  is therefore debited

### C

Emphasise the following:

#### Credit purchase of an Asset

##### Creditors Control

##### Trading stock

\[Left-hand side] Asset increased because the stock has increased|\[Right side]

#### Credit purchase of an Expense

##### Creditors Control

\[Left side]|\[Right side] Liability increased because debt to a creditor increased

##### Stationery

#### Payments of the Creditor's account

##### Creditors Control

\[Left side] Liability decreased because debt to a creditor decreased; \[Right side]

### D

Review the Creditors Ledger and emphasise where we get the 

information from:

CJ; Invoice; Entry in the credit column; Add amount to the balance

CPJ; Cheque; 	Entry in the debit column; Subtract amount from the balance

### E

The business can settle creditors’ accounts in three ways:

1. Settle accounts on the first of the month, for example 1 April: 

Business pays the previous month’s account in full to the 

creditor

2\. Settle accounts in full (up to date): Business pays the full 

amount that he owes up until that day in full

3\. Settle accounts in part: Business only pays a part of the 

creditor’s account.

### F

Discuss the effect of credit transactions on the accounting equation. 

Start with cash purchases and move on to credit purchases.

## Archetypes

### 1

Complete the following sentences.

a) A credit transaction is a transaction where the business buys 

items on \_.

b) A creditor is a \_ because the business must pay back the debt within one year.

c) After the business orders goods, it receives an \_ with the goods.

d) \_ are suppliers to whom the business owes money.

##### Answer

a) A credit transaction is a transaction where the business buys

items on {credit}.

b) A creditor is a {Current liability} because the business must pay back the debt within one year.

c) After the business orders goods, it receives an {original invoice} with the goods.

d) {Creditors} are suppliers to whom the business owes money.
