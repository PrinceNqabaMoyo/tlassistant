Shared invariants / accounting rules
-----------------------------------
1) Trading stock is recorded at COST price in the Trading Stock account.
2) Postings expected (typical):
   - Debit (Dr) side: opening balance b/d, purchases (cash & credit), returns from debtors at cost.
   - Credit (Cr) side: returns to creditors, cost of sales (cash sales + credit sales), drawings, deficits, etc.
   - Balance c/d is closing stock.
3) Returns percentage conventions:
   - Returns to creditors % = (returns to creditors / credit purchases) × 100.
   - Returns from debtors % = (returns from debtors at sales price OR cost price?) × 100.
     NOTE: In our generator, returns from debtors in the Trading Stock account are at cost price.
     If the question wants sales returns %, it must explicitly state “sales returns” and provide sales price.
4) Mark-up on cost:
   - If mark-up is x% on cost, then selling price = cost × (1 + x/100).
   - So cost = selling price / (1 + x/100).
5) Trade discount:
   - If amount PAID is after trade discount d%, then original (before discount) = paid / (1 − d/100).


Archetype TS-A: Section 3 multi-part analysis (Typed)
-----------------------------------------------------
Purpose:
- A multi-part conceptual + calculation question (no table), aligned with the “analysis” style in the docs.

Output type:
- typed question: question_type="typed"

Prompt template:
- Business name + Month/Year.
- A numbered list (3.1 … 3.9 or similar) asking for:
  - Contra accounts for typical trading stock postings.
  - Where closing stock is shown in financial statements.
  - Source documents / journals for specific postings (CPJ/CJ/DAJ/CAJ/CRJ/DJ).
  - One small mark-up conversion (e.g., sales amount given, mark-up given, ask for cost).

Recommended Q items (minimum):
- Contra account for cash purchases of trading stock.
- Contra account for credit purchases of trading stock.
- Contra account for returns to creditors.
- Contra account for returns from debtors recorded at cost.
- “Where is closing trading stock shown?”
- Mark-up conversion: e.g. goods sold for R27 000 on credit; mark-up 25% on cost; calculate cost of sales.

Answer:
- Provide a sample answer with correct formulas and short phrases.

Random data:
- Selling price amount: choose multiple of 100/50.
- Mark-up: choose from [20, 25, 30, 40, 50].


Archetype TS-B: Section 4 prepare Trading Stock account + TWO return percentages
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose:
- A full Trading Stock account preparation (journal/table question)
- PLUS calculation of two return percentages.

Output type:
- journal question: question_type="journal"
- journal_type="trading_stock_account"

Data generation:
1) Generate realistic totals:
   - credit purchases (CJ) > 0
   - returns to creditors (CAJ) >= 0 (usually smaller)
   - returns from debtors at cost (DAJ) >= 0
   - cash purchases (CPJ) > 0
   - cost of sales cash (CRJ) > 0
   - cost of sales credit (DJ) > 0
   - opening stock > 0
2) Use existing helper that creates the Trading Stock account table rows and correct_map.

Prompt requirements:
- Provide a “Journal totals (casting)” block.
- Then “Required:”
  1) Prepare and balance the Trading Stock account.
  2) Calculate % returns to creditors on credit purchases.
  3) Calculate % returns from debtors on cost of sales.
     NOTE: Since DAJ returns in Trading Stock account are cost, base this % on total cost of sales
     (cash + credit) unless the curriculum explicitly says otherwise.

Answer expectations:
- The table correct_map must include all journal postings and balancing.
- Provide working_map/cell_hints (optional scaffold mode).
- Provide calculation guidance in the prompt or guidelines:
  - returns_to_creditors% = (CAJ / CJ)×100
  - returns_from_debtors% = (DAJ / (CRJ+DJ))×100   (cost-based)

Implementation note:
- If the platform supports separate answer fields, include correct values in “correct_map” or
  add a second typed/calc subquestion. If not, embed the percentages as extra editable fields
  (e.g., a separate “calc” question after the journal).


Archetype TS-C: Section 5 markup + trade discount calculations (Typed or Calc)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------
Purpose:
- A calculation-focused item combining mark-up and trade discount.

Output type:
- Prefer "calc" if supported; otherwise "typed" with explicit calculation steps in sample answer.

Prompt options (choose one):
Option 1 (two-step):
1) A supplier invoice list price is given, a trade discount % is given, ask for cost price.
2) Mark-up % on cost is given, ask for selling price.

Option 2 (reverse):
1) Amount paid after trade discount is given, discount % given, ask for original purchases.
2) Then mark-up given, ask for selling price.

Random data:
- Paid/price: multiples of 50/100.
- Discount %: [5, 10, 15].
- Mark-up %: [25, 30, 50].

Answer:
- Show formula and final amounts.
