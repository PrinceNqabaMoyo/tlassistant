## Circles, angles and angular movement



### Foundation

DIAGRAM: A circle showing the circumference, arc, radius, diameter, centre, and sector.



Circumference: The length around the entire edge of a circle is called the circumference. The length of the circumference of a circle is equal to the radius of the circle multiplied by twice the value of pi (C = 2πr or Dπ).



Diameter:  A straight line from one point on the circumference of a circle through the centre to the opposite point on the circumference is called a diameter.



Radius:  A line segment joining the centre of a circle to a point on the circle is called a radius. The diameter is twice the length of the radius of a circle.



Arc:  Part of the circumference of a circle is called an arc.



Sector:  Part of a circle enclosed by two radii of a circle and their intercepted arc, a pie-shaped part of a circle.



Central angle:  A central angle is a positive angle whose vertex is at the centre of a circle.



Standard position of an angle: We measure angles on a circle starting at the positive horizontal axis and moving anti-clockwise unless instructed otherwise.



Concentric circles are circles with the same centre but different radii.

### Measuring angles: Degrees (°)

* A revolution has a measure of 360°.
* An angle of 1° is equal to 1/360 (one three hundred and sixtieth) of a revolution.
* An angle of 1° is equivalent to 60 minutes. We represent 60 minutes as 60'.1° = 60' .So, 1 minute, written as 1', is equivalent to 1/60 (one sixtieth) of an angle of 1°. 
* There are 60 seconds in a minute. We represent 60 seconds as 60". One second (1") is 1/60 (one sixtieth) of a minute and 1/3 600 (one three thousand six hundredth) of an angle of 1°.



##### Examples

= 130° + 49' + (0,86' × 60)

= 130°49'52"

### Radians

A radian is the measure of a central angle that subtends an arc with a length equal to the circle’s radius.

The ratio of the arc length to the radius of the circle: 

•	 Angle (θ) = arc length (s)/radius (r) (s and r must be expressed in the same unit).

•	 Algebraically we write θ = s/r, where θ is in radians.

•	 The number of radii one can fit into a certain arc



C= 2πr, which means that 360 subtends an arc of 2πr. The angle in radians that subtends 2πr is 2πr/r = 2π. 

∴ 360° = 2π rad

We read 2π rad as 2 pi radians. What we mean by this is that there are 2π radians in a revolution. 



The equivalent degrees of 1 radian, is: 1 rad = 360°/2π = 180°/π = 57,296° 

The equivalent radian of 1 degree is:  1° = 2π/360° = π/180° rad 



To convert from radians to degrees we multiply radians by 180°/π

To convert from degrees to radians we multiply degrees by π/180°



##### Examples

1. Problem: Express 2π/3 in terms of degrees.

Solution: 2π/3 = 2π/3 × 180°/π  = 120°



2\. Problem: Convert 20° to radians

Solution: 20° = 20° × π/180° = π/9 rad
