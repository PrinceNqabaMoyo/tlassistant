## NOTES

\-CRJ and CPJ sometimes do not have fol columns

\-Sometimes the Creditors' control has Payments and Discount Received columns nested or grouped under it instead of a Creditors control column and a Discount received column.

\-Sometimes  the Debtors' control has Receipts and Discount allowed columns nested/ grouped under it

* When the transactions says that an amount was put directly into the bank account of the business, then the amount is NOT recorded in the analysis of transactions column. Collorary  that is seems credit card sales are considered direct into the bank and not put in transaction analysis?
*  Some times the CPJ has an EFT column instead of a DOC column
* Sometimes questions explicilty say that the totals should not the cast off/ totalled
* Sometimes a CPJ has a Debtors control for goods sold on credit?
* If different transactions like B/S statements are on the same day, it may be safer for students to keep them separate in the bank column instead of adding.  it avoids complications when they have to seperate amounts with different details in the sundry columns. Alternatively if e.g transactions of the same day can be grouped in the same sundry detail such as Bank charges, they they can be added in the bank entry
* Sometimes the doc corresponding to sales is CRT cash register tape, and sometimes CRR cash register roll
* Loans are also to be included in cash receipt journal questions.
* CPJs sometimes require Wages and/or Equipment columns and sometimes do not.
* What does RD mean?
