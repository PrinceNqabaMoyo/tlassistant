### Financial statements of Partnerships

The basic principles of accounting, the accounting cycle and the accounting equation apply to all businesses. It does not matter if it is a sole trader, partnership or company.

Owners are still entitled to the profit and owner's equity is still the amount owed by the business to the owner/ owners or the owner's interest in the business. The main differences are the distribution of the profit and the structure of the owner's equity.



#### Profit calculation and distribution

The final accounts, Trading account and Profit and loss account, are prepared to calculate the net profit. In a sloe trader the net profit is transferred to the Capital account. In a partnership the final accounts are prepared but the net profit is transferred to an **Appropriation account**. Appropriation means profit sharing. In the Appropriation account, the net profit is divided according to the partnership agreement. The agreement includes details on:

* Interest on capital of each partner
* Salaries of partners
* Bonuses to partners
* Share of remaining profit.



Each partner's profit share is transferred to the Current account of the partner.



Earnings of partner = Interest on capital + Salary of partner + Bonus to partner + part of remaining profit



Owner's equity = Capital of partner + Balance of current account = Capital of partner + (Previous earnings + Earnings - Drawings)
