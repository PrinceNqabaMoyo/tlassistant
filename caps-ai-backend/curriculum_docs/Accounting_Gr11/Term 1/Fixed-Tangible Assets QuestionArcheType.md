## Notes and examples

## Fixed/Tangible Assets

### What is an asset?

Assets are resources that the business possesses and control. They are expected to bring future economic benefits.



#### Assets

##### Non-current assets

These are long-lasting resources (possessions of the business) like property, buildings, vehicles and equipment:



Fixed/Tangible assets, Financial assets: Land and buildings, Vehicle, Equipment



Financial assets: Fixed assets, Investments and Shares in companies.





#### Current assets

These are assets consisting  of cash or that can be converted into cash in the short term (less than a year):

* Stock
* Trade and other receivable/ Debtors
* Cash and cash equivalents



#### Fixed assets

The business uses fixed assets to generate income. Some fixed assets have a limited useful life. They can be used for a certain number of years, after which they must be replaced. The cost of the fixed asset is distributed over its useful life by means of depreciation.



#### Concepts

##### Cost price

The cost price of an asset is the original paid for the asset. This includes any costs incurred in acquiring the asset, such as transport costs, registration costs, installation fees.



##### Depreciation

This is the allocation of the value of the asset over its useful life.

* It is an indication of how much of the value of the asset has been 'used' to generate income in a specific period.
* It indicates which part of the cost of the asset can be used as a tax deductible expense. Two methods of writing off depreciation:

1. Fixed percentage on cost price or straight line method (also called the fixed amount method)
2. Diminished balance method or percentage on carrying value.



##### Accumulated depreciation

This is the total depreciation written off on a specific asset up to a specific date or the total value used.



##### Carrying value

* The remaining value of an asset
* The part of the cost of an asset not yet written off as an expense
* Cost price minus accumulated depreciation
* The minimum carrying value, to be used in the records, is R1.



##### Asset register

An asset register contains all the details of each asset ands assists the business to keep track of each fixed asset's correct value. It is important for the control over assets and decision about the economic value of assets.

#### Disposal of a fixed asset

* If an asset does not offer any more economic benefit to the business, it is better to sell it to prevent any unnecessary cost.
* Any asset can be sold, withdrawn  by owner, traded in another asset or donated
* The following information about the specific assets is needed:

1. The cost price of the asset (from the records)
2. The accumulated depreciation of an asset up to the date
3. The amount for which the asset was sold, traded in or withdrawn (keep a record of this)
4. Any profit or loss made with the transaction (keep a record).

* Students have to know the appropriate rows and their labels.
* In some cases, some row labels are left out and so the students have to put the appropriate row label and then input the appropriate figures and do any necessary calculations.
