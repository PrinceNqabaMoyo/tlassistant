## SECTION A



### QUESTION 1 \[14 marks; 13 minutes]



Read the following statement and answer the questions that follow.

‘Promoting entrepreneurship in South Africa will reduce unemployment.’



#### 1.1

Explain entrepreneurship.  (3)



##### Answer

Entrepreneurship is to invest, organise and run your own business,

accepting the risks involved, with the main objective being to

make a profit.



#### 1.2

Explain why unemployment will reduce if entrepreneurship is

promoted in South Africa.  (2)



##### Answer

When we promote entrepreneurship, more people will start their

own businesses. This will make them economically independent

and active. They will provide work for themselves as well as for

other people, which will reduce unemployment.



#### 1.3

Give THREE characteristics of a good entrepreneur. (3)



##### Answer

Takes risks; confident; energetic; creative thinker; curious; good

at planning; looks out for great opportunities; has a vision of

success; is responsible; is resourceful; works hard; links up with

people who think in the same way and who can help; thinks

critically about challenges; tries out new ideas; is brave; has a good

imagination; has lots of energy for all the long hours he or she

needs to work; enjoys being with people; wants to serve others;

never gives up

&#x20;

#### 1.4

Define the following terms:

##### 1.4.1

Market research  (2)



###### Answer

Market research: research that involves asking your target

market what they want. The information will help you to

decide what good or service to provide.



##### 1.4.2

Competitors  (2)



###### Answer

Competitors: businesses that offer the same good or service as

you do in the same area



##### 1.4.3

Profit  (2)



###### Answer

Profit: when a business sells a good or service for more money

than it costs to produce and sell that good or service

### QUESTION 4	\[18	marks; 16 minutes]

Read the following case study and answer the questions that follow.



'Roger Gumede owns a small shop in the Welgeleë Shopping Centre  that specialises in buying and selling computer games. He is highly motivated and has a solid client base as he is always willing to find new games for his clients. Roger has no experience when it comes to accounting and therefore he has to appoint a bookkeeper. In order to keep his prices as low as possible, he has to add a 30%

mark up to the cost price of each game.

The management of the Welgeleë Shopping Centre has offered

Roger larger premises from where he can run his business. He has been informed that PNA and Checkers will also be selling computer games.'



#### 4.1

Explain what SWOT stands for in a SWOT analysis. (4)



##### &#x20;Answer

Strengths: Strong points;

Weaknesses: Weak points;

Opportunities: Chances;

Threats: Risks



#### 4.2

Prepare a SWOT analysis for Roger. Show two aspects under

each element.  (8)



##### Answer

STRENGTHS: Motivated; solid client base

WEAKNESSES: No knowledge of accounting; mark up is too low

OPPORTUNITIES: Opportunity to expand; sell more products

THREATS: Other shops that sell the same product at a lower price



#### 4.3

List ONE element of the SWOT analysis that Roger has control

over and ONE element that he has no control over. (2)



##### Answer

Control: Strengths/Weaknesses ✔

No control: Opportunities/Threats ✔



#### 4.4

List TWO things that Roger can do so that he doesn’t lose his

clients to PNA and Checkers.  (2)



##### Answer

&#x20;Stock a larger variety of games; give a more personal service and

better advice



#### 4.5

What is the biggest disadvantage for Roger’s business if he

moves to larger premises? (2)



##### Answer

Rent will increase; other costs will increase.





### QUESTION 5 \[19 marks; 17 minutes]

Sibu runs a pizzeria in a local shopping complex. He did not have the opportunity to study EMS at school and has asked you to help him calculate his costs. The information below refers to the Hawaiian pizza that he sells.

&#x20;

#### 5.1

Explain to Sibu what variable costs are.  (2)



##### Answer

These are costs that change according to how many goods a

business produces. Variable costs increase when more goods are

produced and decrease when fewer goods are produced



#### 5.2

Calculate the costs of making one pizza, keeping the following

in mind: (6)



INGREDIENTS; COSTS

PIZZA bases; R28 for 4

Cheese; R70 per Kg (each pizza requires 100g)

Tomatoes; R10 per can (one can make two pizzas)

Ham; R100 per Kg (each pizza requires 50g)

Pineapple; R12 per (one can make six pizzas)



##### Answer

Pizza base				R7 ✔

Cheese					R7 ✔

Tomatoes					R5 ✔

Ham						R5 ✔

Pineapple					R2 ✔

TOTAL FOR ONE PIZZA:	R26 ✔



#### 5.3

Calculate the labour costs per pizza by using the following

information: (2)

* There is one employee who earns R60 per hour.
* It takes 10 minutes to make one pizza.



##### Answer

&#x20;R60 ÷ 6 = R10 per pizza



#### 5.4

Explain to Sibu what fixed costs are. (2)



##### Answer

These are costs that you have to pay each month and that do not change, regardless of how many goods are produced



#### 5.5

List TWO fixed costs that Sibu probably has. (4)



##### Answer

Rent for the shop; salaries and wages for employees



#### 5.6

If Sibu sells one pizza for R52, what is his mark up?

Show your calculation. (3)

Mark up = selling price – cost price

&#x20; = R52 – R26

&#x20; = R26 per pizza ✔✔✔
