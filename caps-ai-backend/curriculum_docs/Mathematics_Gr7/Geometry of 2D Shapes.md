### Different types of triangles

##### equilateral, isosceles, scalene and right-angled triangles



A triangle with two equal sides is called an isosceles triangle. The 2 angles that the sides make with the 3rd side are also equal to each other.

A triangle with three equal sides is called an equilateral triangle. All 3 angles are also equal

A triangle with a right angle is called a right-angled triangle.

A triangle with three sides with different lengths and no right angle is called a scalene triangle. All three angles are also of different sizes.

DIAGRAM\[Circle with red circumference, centre is marked M. Point A on the circumference is place so that radius AM is drawn in blue. Point B is on the circumference so that chord AB is drawn in black]

A straight line from the midpoint of a circle to a point on the circle is called a radius of the circle.

The blue line, MA, is a radius. Any straight line from the centre to the circle is a radius.

The black line AB joins two points on the circle. We call this line a chord of the circle.



DIAGRAM\[2 Circles. The first circle has red circumference, centre M and points A and B on the circumference AM is drawn in blue, MB and chord AB are drawn in black. Radius MB is extended by a dotted line to form a diameter. The half circle without chord AB is shaded blue.]

In the two diagrams above, the coloured sections are segments of a 

circle. A segment is the area between a chord and an arc.



DIAGRAM\[Pink circle showing a sector shaded in pink]

In the circle above, the red section is called a sector of a circle. As you can see, a sector is the region between two radii and an arc.
