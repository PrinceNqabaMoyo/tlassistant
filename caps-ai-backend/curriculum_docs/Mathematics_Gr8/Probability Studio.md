&nbsp;Probability experiments

&nbsp;In the Intermediate Phase and Grade 7 learners did probability experiments with 

coins, dice and spinners. In Grade 8 doing actual trials of experiments become 

less important, and learners should consider probability for hypothetical events 

e.g. the probability of white as a successful outcome on a roulette table, or the 

probability of getting a Coca Cola at the shop if you know what the total number 

of drinks is that they stock and how many cans of Coca Cola they have.

&nbsp;Comparing relative frequency and probability

&nbsp;• The relative frequency is the observed number of successful outcomes for a 

finite sample of trials. 

• For example, if you toss a coin 50 times, the results are 27 heads and 23 tails. 

Define a head as a successful outcome. The relative frequency of heads is:  

27/50 = 54%

&nbsp;• The probability of a head is 50% (one of two likely outcomes). The difference 

between the relative frequency of 54% and the probability of 50% is due to 

small sample size.

&nbsp;• The more trials you do, the closer the relative frequency gets to the probability. 

This can be compared in class by combining results from trials done in groups 

or pairs.
