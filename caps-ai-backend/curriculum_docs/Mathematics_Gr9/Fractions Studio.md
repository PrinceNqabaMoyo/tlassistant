##### converting between mixed numbers and fractions

Numbers that have both whole number and fraction parts are called mixed numbers.

Examples of mixed numbers: 3 4/5, 2 7/8 and 8 3/10.

Mixed numbers can be written in expanded notation, for example:

3 4/5 means 3 + 4/5; 2 7/8 means 2 + 7/8;  8 3/10 means 8 + 3/10.



To add and subtract mixed numbers, you can work with the whole number parts and the fraction parts separately, for example:

3 4/5 + 13 3/5 = 16 7/5 = 17 2/5

13 3/5 - 3 4/5 = 10 -1/5 = 9 4/5



However, this method can be difficult to do with some examples, and it does not work with multiplication and division.

An alternative and preferred method is to convert the mixed number to an improper fraction, as shown in the example below:

3 4/5 = 3 + 4/5 = 15/5 + 4/5 = 19/5.



NOTE

You can obtain the numerator of 19 in one step by multiplying the

denominator (5) by the whole number (3), and then adding the numerator (4).



So, you can calculate 3 4/5 + 13 3/5 using this method:

3 4/5 + 13 3/5

= 19/5 + 68/5

= 87/5

The answer must be converted to a mixed number again: 87/5 = 17 2/5.

### Adding and subtracting fractions

To add or subtract two fractions, they have to be expressed with the same denominators first. To achieve that, one or more of the given fractions may have to be replaced with equivalent fractions.



3/20 + 2/5 = 3/20 + (2×4)/(5×4)  \[to get twentieths] 

= 3/20 + 8/20 = 11/20



5/12 + 7/20 = (5×20)/(12×20) + (7×12)/(20×12) =100/240 + 84/240 =184/240 = 23/30



We will later refer to this method of adding or subtracting fractions as Method A.



In the case of 5/12 + 7/20, multiplying by 20 and by 12 was a sure way of making equivalent fractions of the same kind, in this case two hundred-and-fortieths. However, the numbers became quite big. Just imagine how big the numbers will become if you use the same method to calculate 17/75 + 13/85!



Fortunately, there is a method of keeping the numbers smaller (in many cases) when making equivalent fractions, so that fractions can be added or subtracted. In this method you first calculate the lowest common multiple or LCM of the denominators. In the case of 5/12 + 7/20, the smaller multiples of the denominators are: 

12: 12; 24; 36; 48; 60; 72; 84

20: 20; 40; 60; 80; 100; 120; 140



The smallest number that is a multiple of both 12 and 20 is 60.

Both 5/12 and 7/20 can be expressed in terms of sixtieths:

5/12 = (5×5)/(12×5) = 25/60, because to make twelfths into sixtieths you have to divide each twelfth into five equal parts, to get 12 × 5 = 60 equal parts, i.e. sixtieths.



Similarly, 7/20 = (7×3)/(20×3) = 21/60.



Hence 5/12 + 7/20 = 25/60 + 21/60 = 46/60 = 23/30.

We may call this method the LCM method of adding or subtracting fractions.

##### Division by a fraction

* When we divide by a fraction, we have a very different situation. Think about this: If you have 40 pizzas, how many learners can have 3/5 a pizza each?

To find the number of fifths in 40 pizzas: 40 × 5 = 200 fifths of a pizza. To find the number of three fifths: 200 ÷ 3 = 66 portions of 3/5 pizza and two fifths of a pizza is left over.

So, to calculate 40 ÷ 3/5, we multiplied by 5 and divided by 3, and that gave us 66 and two thirds of a portion. In fact, we calculated 40 × 5/3.



* Division is the inverse of multiplication. So, to divide by a fraction, you multiply by its inverse.



Example: 18/50 ÷ 2/3 = 18/60 × 3/2 = 54/120 = 9/20
