### The equation of the tangent to a circle

&nbsp;A tangent is a line that touches a circle at one point. A tangent line to a circle is perpendicular 

to the radius. We know that the product of the gradients of perpendicular lines is negative one.

### &nbsp;Intersecting circles and straight lines

that satisfy both the circle and the straight line, that is, the coordinates are the same. Hence, 

the equations are solved simultaneously

### &nbsp;The ellipse

&nbsp;An ellipse is the set of all points that make an oval shape. 

There are two main types of ellipses:  

a) The horizontal major axis ellipse

b) and the vertical major axis ellipse.



###### Diagram:

&nbsp;horizontal major axis ellipse-:  intersects the ellipse at the vertices  

(± a; 0) and the line perpendicular to the major axis at the center intersects the ellipse at the co

vertices (0, ± b).  This line is called the minor axis of the ellipse.



vertical major axis ellipse-: looks like the horizontal major axis turned 90 degrees about the origin.



General equation of the horizontal major axis ellipse is x^2/b^2 + y^2/a^2 = 1, Where:

&nbsp;•  (x; y)  are the co-ordinates of any point on the ellipse

&nbsp;  •  a, b  are the radii on the  x and y  axes respectively

&nbsp;For a horizontal ellipse:

&nbsp;• the major axis has length 2a.  

&nbsp;• the minor axis has length 2b.  

&nbsp;To find the foci which is denoted by (c) we use the formula c =  √(a^2 −  b^2)  



The general equation of the vertical major axis ellipse is  x^2/a^2 + y^2/b^2 = 1
