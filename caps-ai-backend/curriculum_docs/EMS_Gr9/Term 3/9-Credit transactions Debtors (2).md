#### 5\.

Debtors can settle their accounts in three ways:

– Settle accounts on the first of the month, for example, 1 April: Debtor pays the previous month’s account in full

– Settle accounts in full (up to date): Debtor pays the full amount that he or she owes up until that day in full

– Settle accounts in part: Debtor pays only a part of his or her account.



#### 6\.

It is difficult to explain an account like Trading Stock with only

half the information. Goods have been sold, but not yet bought,

so where do the goods come from? Maybe in expanations or visual aids two half completed accounts have to be added to do the Trading Stock account in full, with entries from the CPJ (cash purchases), the CRJ (the cost price of goods sold for cash) and the DJ (the cost price of goods sold on credit).



#### 7\.

The Trading Stock and Debtors Control accounts are key accounts

and the relationship between them must be clearly understood.

Write the following on the board and explain the two accounts

to learners or use a specific figure in the activity and ask them to

explain what the figure means. Sometimes if learners understand it

in this way, the content becomes more real to them.

#### 9\.

Cost of sales from DJ

Sales from CRJ

#### 10

* Sales is an income account and income increases when goods are sold. ✔ Income accounts increase by credit entries. ✔
* Trading stock is an Asset and this asset decreases by the cost price of goods sold, as the stock is no longer in the business. Asset accounts decrease by credit entries. ✔
* Cost of sales is an Expense and increases when more goods are sold. ✔ Expense accounts increase by debit entries. ✔
