# 1

“Inquiry-based learning transforms the classroom into a space where curiosity drives discovery, and learners become active creators of knowledge rather than passive recipients.”



## Summary with Attribution

Walusa and Qhosola-Mahlomaholo (2025) argue that inquiry-based learning fosters deeper engagement by encouraging learners to question, investigate, and construct their own understanding of accounting concepts. Their study demonstrates that this method not only enhances critical thinking and problem-solving but also nurtures independence and confidence in learners. By positioning students as active participants in knowledge creation, inquiry-based approaches prepare them for both academic success and real-world challenges.



## Reference

Walusa, A., \& Qhosola-Mahlomaholo, M. R. (2025). Enhancing critical thinking and problem-solving skills of grade 11 learners in accounting education in high school at uMgungundlovu district, South Africa. International Journal of Studies in Inclusive Education, 2(3), 64–71. https://doi.org/10.38140/ijsie.v2i3.2169



# 2

“Problem-based learning is perhaps the most innovative instructional method conceived in the history of education, and its most consistent finding is the superiority of PBL-trained learners in life-long learning.” (Hung, Jonassen, \& Liu, 2008, cited in Gerstein, Winter, \& Hertz, 2016)



## Summary with Attribution

Gerstein, Winter, and Hertz (2016) argue that problem-based learning (PBL) is uniquely effective in teaching accounting ethics because it engages students in real-world dilemmas, fosters collaboration, and cultivates self-directed learning. They highlight that PBL equips learners with flexible thinking, critical reasoning, and intrinsic motivation — skills essential for ethical decision-making in professional practice. By grounding ethics education in inquiry and problem-solving, the authors contend that PBL not only enhances academic outcomes but also nurtures moral imagination, preparing future accountants to act responsibly in complex business environments.



## Reference

Gerstein, M., Winter, E., \& Hertz, S. (2016). Teaching Accounting Ethics: A Problem-Based Learning Approach. Journal of Accounting, Ethics \& Public Policy, 17(1), 102–126. Retrieved from SSRN: https://ssrn.com/abstract=2732093

# 4

“Students reported increased confidence, improved problem-solving skills, and greater engagement in the learning process… the inquiry-based methodology has helped me become a more confident and independent learner.”



## Summary with Attribution

Martinez-Blasco, Markulin, and Bosch (2025) present a structured inquiry-based approach in accounting education through Study and Research Paths (SRPs). Their findings across three implementations reveal that students not only engaged more deeply with accounting concepts but also demonstrated enhanced teamwork, autonomy, and problem-solving abilities. Surveys and interviews confirmed that learners valued the real-world relevance of tasks, reported improved confidence, and appreciated the shift toward active, self-directed learning. The authors argue that SRPs provide a replicable model for embedding inquiry-based learning in accounting, ultimately leading to better performance outcomes and stronger professional readiness.



## Reference

Martinez-Blasco, M., Markulin, K., \& Bosch, M. (2025). A proposal for inquiry-based learning in accounting using study and research paths. Cogent Education, 12(1), 2547942. https://doi.org/10.1080/2331186X.2025.2547942



# 5

“Student-question-based inquiry supports the learning of inquiry skills, improves discussion and reasoning, increases motivation, and strengthens confidence and ownership of learning.” (Herranen \& Aksela, 2019)



## Summary with Attribution

Herranen and Aksela (2019) conducted a systematic review of 30 studies on student-question-based inquiry in science education. Their findings show that when students’ own questions drive inquiry, learners demonstrate improved problem-solving, reasoning, and discussion skills. The approach also enhances motivation, engagement, and confidence, while fostering a sense of ownership over learning. The authors emphasize the teacher’s critical role in scaffolding and guiding inquiry, but conclude that student-question-based inquiry (SQBI) is a powerful model for improving performance and cultivating lifelong learning skills.



## Reference

Herranen, J., \& Aksela, M. (2019). Student-question-based inquiry in science education. Studies in Science Education. Advance online publication.
